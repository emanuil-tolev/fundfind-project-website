FundFind Project

For better, open, easier funding of scholarship

FundFind

The FundFind website has actual funding data and runs on the fundfind software.

This site is about the fundfind software itself.

What?

FundFind is a piece of software which allows users to share funding opportunities with each other and search through them.

Scholars can share data with each other or explore funding in their field. Funders can submit their opportunities or improve the records, as well as explore what other funders are offering.

How?

Why?

Because there was nothing which encourages publishing current (not historical) funding data openly.

There are commercial databases which allow scholars to look for funding opportunities. However, these require whole institutions to agree to expensive licensing terms. But this data should be Open in line with the Open Definition principles and the general Open Knowledge movement.

Public information

Most of scholarship (in the UK) is funded by public bodies. Something as essential as what funding is available should be available in an openly licensed, machine-friendly format.

A better, innovative sector

Open funding data means a better deal for scholars (transparency, potential for huge time savings) as well as funders (reach more scholars).

It also means a better scholarship sector overall, with a transparent flow of money and plenty of opportunities for visualisation, exploration, analysis and ultimately, improvement of the sector. A lot of potential innovation is simply not happening now.

Allowing exploration

Young scholars find it difficult to learn about funding in their field as it is difficult to navigate funding information - it is fragmented across multiple funders' websites and no overviews are available.

The scholarly funding sector could use a gentle touch of modernisation, removing some of the obstacles which impede learning.
